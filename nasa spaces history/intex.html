Artemis I:

Overview

Artemis I was a groundbreaking uncrewed mission launched by NASA in November 2022. It served as a crucial test flight for the agency's Artemis program, which aims to return humans to the Moon by the end of the 2020s.

Details

Details

Launch and landing:

Ingenuity was launched to Mars with the Perseverance rover on July 30, 2020. It landed on the Martian surface on February 18, 2021, attached to the belly of the rover.

First flight:

Ingenuity made its first successful flight on April 19, 2021, hovering for 30 seconds at an altitude of 10 feet (3 meters).

Subsequent flights:

Ingenuity has made several successful flights since its first, demonstrating the feasibility of powered flight on Mars. It has explored the Martian landscape, captured images, and collected data about the atmosphere.

Mission objectives

Ingenuity's primary mission is to demonstrate the capabilities of powered flight on Mars. It is also serving as a technology demonstrator for future Mars missions, testing technologies that could be used for aerial exploration and transportation.

Lucy Mission

Overview

The Lucy Mission is a NASA spacecraft designed to study the Trojan asteroids of Jupiter. These asteroids are located in two stable regions along Jupiter's orbit, known as the L4 and L5 Lagrange points.

Details

Launch

Lucy was launched on October 13, 2021, atop a United Launch Alliance Atlas V rocket.

Mission objectives

Lucy's primary objectives are to: Explore the diversity of the Trojan asteroids.Study the formation and evolution of the solar system. Investigate the origins of Earth-like planets.

Target asteroids:

Lucy is scheduled to visit seven Trojan asteroids during its mission, including five never-before-explored asteroids.

Mission duration:

The Lucy mission is expected to last 12 years, with the final flyby scheduled for 2033.

Psyche Mission

Overview

The Psyche Mission is a NASA spacecraft designed to explore the asteroid 16 Psyche. This asteroid is a unique object in the solar system, as it is believed to be the core of a planet that was stripped of its outer layers.

Details

Launch:

Psyche is scheduled to launch in 2026. Mission objectives: Psyche's primary objectives are to: Study the composition and structure of the asteroid. Investigate the formation and evolution of planetary cores. Gain insights into the early history of our solar system.

Target asteroid:

16 Psyche is a metal-rich asteroid located in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.

Flybys:

Psyche will perform flybys of the asteroid, collecting data on its physical properties, composition, and magnetic field.

Mission duration:

The Psyche mission is expected to last 21 months.
