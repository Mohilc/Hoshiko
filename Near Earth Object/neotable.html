NEO Close-Approach Data

1. Object Name or Designation

The official designation of the NEO, such as (2023 TG14) or (2007 UT3). These can be provisional names assigned when the object is first discovered.

2. Close-Approach Date and Time

The specific date and time when the NEO will come closest to Earth, typically listed in UTC.

Example: 2024-Oct-24 23:34

3. Nominal Distance (AU)

The expected distance between the NEO and Earth at closest approach, measured in Astronomical Units (AU).

Example: 0.01681 AU (about 2.5 million kilometers).

4. Minimum Possible Distance (AU)

The minimum possible distance that could occur, factoring in uncertainties in the NEO’s orbit.

Example: 0.01605 AU

5. Relative Velocity (km/s)

The speed at which the NEO will pass Earth, measured in kilometers per second.

Example: 6.90 km/s

6. Orbit Intersection Distance (AU)

This shows how closely the orbit of the NEO intersects with Earth's orbit.

Example: 0.01605 AU

7. Magnitude (H)

The absolute magnitude of the object, which is related to its brightness and size.

Example: 25.8 H

8. Estimated Diameter

An estimate of the NEO’s size based on its brightness and reflectivity, usually provided as a range.

Example: 18 m - 40 m

9. Condition Code

A value from 0 to 9 that indicates the uncertainty in the NEO’s orbit. Lower numbers indicate higher confidence.

Example: 0 (high confidence)

10. Earth MOID (Minimum Orbit Intersection Distance)

The smallest distance between the orbit of the NEO and Earth’s orbit, helping assess future impact risks.

Example: 0.01605 AU
