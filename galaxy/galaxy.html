Milky Way

Overview

The Milky Way galaxy is a vast spiral galaxy that is home to our solar system. It is estimated to contain hundreds of billions of stars and is about 100,000 light-years in diameter.

Informations

Rotation

The Milky Way is constantly rotating. Our solar system orbits the galaxy's center once every 230 million years.

Star Formation

New stars are constantly being formed in the Milky Way, primarily in regions of dense gas and dust.

Supermassive Black Hole:

: At the center of the Milky Way lies a supermassive black hole, known as Sagittarius A*, which has a mass of about 4 million times that of our sun.

Dark Matter

The majority of the Milky Way's mass is thought to be made up of dark matter, a mysterious substance that does not interact with light.

Andromeda Galaxy (M31)

Overview

Andromeda is the most distant object visible to the naked eye. It was first cataloged by the Persian astronomer Al-Sufi in the 10th century. The Andromeda Galaxy was once thought to be a nebula within our own Milky Way. The collision between the Milky Way and Andromeda will create a giant elliptical galaxy. This information is based on current scientific understanding and may be subject to change as new discoveries are made.

Details

Distance from Earth

Estimated diameter of 2.5 km (1.6 mi)

Size

Roughly twice the size of the Milky Way, with an estimated diameter of around 220,000 light-years.

Shape

A barred spiral galaxy, similar to the Milky Way.

Movement:

The Andromeda Galaxy is moving towards the Milky Way at a speed of about 110 kilometers per second. The two galaxies are expected to collide and merge in about 4.5 billion years.

The Whirlpool Galaxy (M51)

Overview

The Whirlpool Galaxy was discovered by Charles Messier in 1773. It is one of the most popular galaxies for amateur astronomers to observe due to its distinctive spiral structure. The Whirlpool Galaxy has been studied extensively by astronomers to learn more about galaxy formation and evolution. This information is based on current scientific understanding and may be subject to change as new discoveries are made.

Details

Distance from Earth

Approximately 23 million light-years.

Size:

About half the size of the Milky Way, with an estimated diameter of around 100,000 light-years.

Shape:

A grand design spiral galaxy with prominent spiral arms.

Star Formation:

The Whirlpool Galaxy is actively forming new stars, particularly in its spiral arms.
