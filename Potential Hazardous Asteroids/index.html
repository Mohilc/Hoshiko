99942 Apophis

Overview

Near-Earth asteroid discovered in 2004. Potentially hazardous object due to its size and proximity to Earth's orbit. Initial concerns about potential impact in 2029, but later ruled out. Small chance of impact in 2036 remains, but highly unlikely. Gravitational keyhole could potentially alter its orbit. NASA's OSIRIS-REx mission planned to study Apophis in 2029

Details

Current Status:

Apophis is currently classified as a potentially hazardous asteroid.

Orbits

Its orbit around the Sun brings it relatively close to Earth.

Impact Risk:

While there was once a small chance of Apophis impacting Earth in 2029, subsequent observations have ruled out this possibility.

Close Flyby:

Apophis will make a very close flyby of Earth in 2029, passing within a few tens of thousands of kilometers.

415029-2011 UL21

Overview

(415029) 2011 UL21 is a potentially hazardous asteroid discovered on October 17, 2011, by the Catalina Sky Survey project. It is estimated to have a diameter of 2.5 kilometers (1.6 mi). It was rated at Torino Scale 1 on October 27, 2011, with an observation arc of 9.6 days. However, as more observations were made, the impact probability was significantly reduced and eventually ruled out.

Details

Size

Estimated diameter of 2.5 km (1.6 mi)

Orbits

Crosses Earth's orbit

Close approach:

Had a relatively close fly-by with Earth on June 27, 2024

Potential hazard

Initially rated at Torino Scale 1, but later ruled out

2019 OK

Overview

67943 Duende

Overview

367943 Duende is a near-Earth asteroid that made a remarkably close approach to Earth on February 15, 2013. It was discovered on February 23, 2012, by astronomers at the La Sagra Sky Survey in Spain.

Details

Size

Approximately 30 meters in diameter, making it a relatively small object.

Orbits

Near-Earth asteroid

Type

An Aten and Atira group asteroid, meaning it has a relatively small orbit that crosses Earth's orbit

Close Approach:

In 2013, it passed within 27,700 kilometers of Earth's surface, a record-breaking distance for an object of its sizes
