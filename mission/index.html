Europa Clipper
Europa Clipper is a robotic solar-powered spacecraft designed to conduct detailed investigations of Jupiter's icy moon Europa.

NASA space mission
International Space Station
The ISS has been a significant milestone in space exploration, demonstrating the potential for international collaboration and the benefits of conducting research in microgravity. It continues to play a vital role in advancing our understanding of the universe and developing technologies for future space missions.
International Space Station
Purpose: A large, low-Earth orbit space station serving as a laboratory for scientific research and technology development.

Partners: Operated by NASA (US), Roscosmos (Russia), ESA (Europe), JAXA (Japan), and CSA (Canada).

Orbit: Orbits Earth approximately every 90 minutes at an altitude of around 250 miles.

Crew: Typically houses a crew of six astronauts or cosmonauts.

Research: Conducts experiments in various fields, including biology, physics, astronomy, and materials science.

Assembly: Constructed in orbit over several years, with modules from different countries being added piece by piece.

NASA SPACE MISSION
NISAR
NISAR stands for NASA-ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar. It's a joint Earth-observing mission between NASA and the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO)..
NISAR
Dual-frequency radar: NISAR will carry two radars, one operating at L-band and the other at S-band. This combination will allow it to observe a wider range of changes on Earth's surface than a single-frequency radar..

Joint mission by NASA and ISRO.

Dual-frequency radar (L-band and S-band).

Global mapping every 12 days.

Studies Earth's surface changes (ecosystems, ice, vegetation, sea level, groundwater, hazards).

Helps understand Earth's processes (climate change, resource management, disaster response).

INDIA SPACE MISSION
chandryaan 3
Chandrayaan-3 is a lunar exploration mission by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). It is the third mission in the Chandrayaan program, following Chandrayaan-1 and Chandrayaan-2. The mission consists of a lander named Vikram and a rover named Pragyan.
chandryaan 3

Launch date: July 14, 2023

Launch site: Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota, India

Objective: To demonstrate end-to-end capability in safe landing and roving on the lunar surface.

Landing site: Near the Moon's south pole

Mission duration: 14 days (lunar day).successfully landed on the Moon on August 23, 2023

INDIA SPACE MISSION
Mangalyaan
The Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), also known as Mangalyaan, was India's first interplanetary mission to Mars. It was launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) on November 5, 2013, and successfully entered Martian orbit on September 24, 2014.
